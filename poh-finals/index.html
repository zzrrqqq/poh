Why should Artists Embrace AI-based Art

Central Claim

"Even though there exist ethical problems with AI-based art, such as bias, job loss, and unclear copyright and responsibility, we should still embrace AI-based art as it is not only a suitable way to explore the technology and solve these problems, but also because it will create a new perspective of art."

Introduction

Generative AI has been a hit these years. Using the technology in areas like business and marketing is widely accepted. However, when it comes to art, especially fine art, things become much more controversial. Some artists consider AI as a scourge, others criticize ethical problems twined with the deep-learning based tools, while the rest holds a much more positive voice and are willing to embrace the technology

Characteristics of AI

Low Creativity

Human intelligence can be divided into analytical intelligence and Intuitive Intelligence. The latter, which involves thinking and feeling is what art emphasize on. However, current technology only enables analytic intelligence, intuitive intelligence is still too complex

High Productivity

Mornet spent seven years in creating "The Great Water Lily", while generative AI only need 10 seconds to create a similar one with the same amount of details. By using this in creating drafts for art pieces, it will save the artists a lot of time and trial and error cost
